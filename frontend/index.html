Stats Legend

PPG: Points Per Game
  • Average number of points one scores per game played
  • 25+ PPG is often considered elite for NBA scorers
APG: Assists Per Game
  • An assist is credited to a player who passes the ball to a teammate in a way that leads to a score
  • 10+ APG is often considered elite for NBA playmakers
RPG: Rebounds Per Game
  • A rebound is credited to a player who retrieves the ball after a missed shot
  • 10+ RPG is often considered elite for NBA rebounders
SPG: Steals Per Game
  • A steal is credited to a player who legally takes the ball away from an opponent or deflects it to cause a turnover
  • 2+ SPG is often considered elite for NBA defenders
BPG: Blocks Per Game
  • A block is credited to a player who deflects the ball away, preventing it from going into the basket
  • 2+ BPG is often considered elite for NBA shot-blockers
TPG: Turnovers Per Game
  • A turnover happens when a player loses possession of the ball to the opposing team, due to an error or a rule violation
  • Lower TPG is preferred; elite players usually aim to minimize their turnovers
FG%: Field Goal Percentage
  • Represents the percentage of field goals (shots) made out of attempts
  • 50% or higher FG% is often considered excellent for NBA players, but varies by position
3FG%: Three-Point Field Goal Percentage
  • Represents the percentage of three-point shots made out of attempts
  • 40% or higher 3FG% is often considered excellent for NBA shooters
